FCSys.UsersGuide.SampleResults.Basic

The figures below show the results from some basic, low-level examples of FCSys. For more information about any of the results, please follow the links to the associated models.

The models have been simulated using Dymola 7.4. The plots have been generated using ModelicaRes and this Python script.

** Reorder, renumber:


Figure 1: ** (FCSys.Subregions.Examples.AirColumn).


Figure 1: Pressure waves reflecting across two 1 cm3 regions with an initial pressure difference. The nonlinearity is due to upstream discretization and the initial condition (FCSys.Subregions.Examples.Echo).


Figure 2: The conditions are the same as for Figure 1, but the central difference scheme is used (FCSys.Subregions.Examples.EchoCentral).


Figure 1: ** (FCSys.Subregions.Examples.ElectricalConduction).


Figure 1: ** (FCSys.Subregions.Examples.ORR).


Figure 1: ** (FCSys.Subregions.Examples.HOR).


Figure 2: Thermal conduction through a graphite bar divided into 1 cm3 subregions (FCSys.Subregions.Examples.ThermalConduction).


Figure 3: Velocity induced in gas in contact with graphite undergoing thermal conduction (FCSys.Subregions.Examples.ThermalConductionConvection).


Figure 4: Validation of the H2O saturation pressure curve derived from Gibbs equilibrium in FCSys, as compared to Modelica.Media.Air.MoistAir (FCSys.Subregions.Examples.SaturationPressure).


Figure 5: Dynamic H2O evaporation and condensation. From 1 to 2 s, additional vapor is injected (FCSys.Subregions.Examples.Evaporation).
